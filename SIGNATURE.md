## The ONE signature moment: "The Daily Drop"
An interactive seven-day price dial. The visitor scrubs across the week
(FRIDAY → THURSDAY) and watches the live store price tick **down** in real time
($6 → $5 → $4 → $3 → $2 → $1), the day's character/copy change ("first pick" vs
"the steal"), and the "today" marker glow on the real current weekday.

Why it wins:
- It IS the pitch, literally. The whole business model is "price drops every day."
- Industry-rare: no bin store has ever turned its price-by-day rule into an
  interactive instrument you can play.
- Calm museum stage for a chaotic hunt — the restraint is the surprise, fits the
  timeless centered lane.
- Degrades gracefully (visible-by-default, reduced-motion safe, snaps to today).

## Distance / Novelty / Escalation
- **Distance:** pine-teal on warm paper + Gambetta + centered symmetrical stage is
  far from recent builds. Signature is a scrubbable price-by-day dial — not a
  reveal-card, braid-draw, breathing ring, dial-thermostat, or EKG line.
- **Novelty:** discount/variety/bin-store sites are loud neon clip-art chaos; we
  do the opposite — a quiet gallery that makes one wild idea (daily price decay)
  feel premium.
- **Escalation:** a real-time, user-driven value-decay instrument tied to the live
  weekday, with bespoke per-day copy and a today-snap.
